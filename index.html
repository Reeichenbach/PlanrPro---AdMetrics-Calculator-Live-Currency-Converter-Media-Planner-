📚 Media Planning Guide

Agency-grade foundations · Read before you calculate

🏗 What is Media Planning?

Media planning is the process of determining where, when, how much, and to whom you will serve paid advertisements — and forecasting exactly what business outcomes that investment will produce.

An agency-grade media plan is not just a budget table. It is a mathematical document that shows: the benchmark assumptions going in, every formula used, and the output metrics that prove the plan is grounded in reality.

A media plan answers 5 questions precisely:
Who are we reaching? · Where will we reach them? · How much will we spend? · What will we get? · How will we measure it?
🔺 The Three Layers of a Professional Media Plan
1
Strategy Layer
Audience, platform, objective, messaging. The why and who.
2
Tactical Layer
Campaign structure, ad formats, bid strategy, geo. The how and when.
3
Forecasting Layer
Budget → Impressions → Clicks → Leads → Revenue. The how much.
⚖️ Bad Plan vs. Good Plan
🔴 Bad Plan says:
"We'll spend ₹2L/month and expect 100 leads." No formulas. No benchmark sources. No funnel breakdown. No KPIs. Just numbers from thin air.
🟢 Good Plan says:
"At a ₹2L budget, assuming CPC of ₹180 (Q1 2026 India Home Loan benchmark), we'll generate 1,111 clicks at 4.2% CTR = 26,462 impressions. At 5.2% CVR we get 57 leads at a CPL of ₹3,508. Formula: Leads = Budget ÷ CPC × CVR."

7️⃣ The 7-Step Media Planning Process

Every professional media plan follows this sequence. Skip a step and your plan will have holes.

  • 1
    Define the Campaign ObjectiveEvery campaign must have ONE primary objective: Awareness (impressions/reach), Consideration (traffic/engagement), or Conversion (leads/sales). This determines platform, format, bid strategy, and KPI.
  • 2
    Define the AudienceWho specifically are you reaching? Age, income, geography, intent signal, device preference, life stage. Each persona = a separate targeting layer in your plan.
  • 3
    Select Platforms & FormatsMatch platform to where your audience is AND their intent level. High commercial intent → Google Search. Interest/demographic targeting → Meta. Remarketing → Google Display + Meta Retargeting.
  • 4
    Set the Budget & Allocate ItTotal budget → split by platform → split by product/campaign → split by city. Justify allocation with search volume (Google) or audience size (Meta), product LTV, and city-level CPC/CPM benchmarks.
  • 5
    Research Benchmark InputsThis is the foundation of your forecast. You MUST source: CPC/CPM benchmarks, CTR benchmarks, CVR benchmarks, and seasonality factors. Always cite your source.
  • 6
    Build the Forecast Using FormulasApply benchmark inputs to your budget using the correct formula chain. Show EVERY step. Never present a final number without the formula that produced it.
  • 7
    Define KPIs, Tracking & ReportingFor each campaign define: primary KPI, secondary KPIs, measurement method (GTM, GA4, CRM), reporting frequency, and the performance threshold that triggers optimization. Without this, the plan has no accountability layer.
🔵 Google Ads — Formula Chain

Google Search is CPC-based. Start with budget, derive clicks, then everything else from clicks.

Budget ÷ CPC = Clickse.g. ₹30,000 ÷ ₹130 = 230 clicks
↓
Clicks ÷ CTR = Impressionse.g. 230 ÷ 0.042 = 5,476 impressions
↓
Clicks × CVR = Leadse.g. 230 × 0.048 = 11 leads
↓
Budget ÷ Leads = CPLe.g. ₹30,000 ÷ 11 = ₹2,727 CPL
Additional Google formulas
CPL shortcut = CPC ÷ CVR% → ₹130 ÷ 0.048 = ₹2,708 ✓
Actual CPC = (Competitor Ad Rank ÷ QS) + ₹0.01
Ad Rank = Max Bid × QS × Extension Impact
🔴 Meta Ads — Formula Chain

Meta is CPM-based. Start with budget, derive impressions, then clicks and leads from impressions.

(Budget ÷ CPM) × 1,000 = Impressionse.g. (₹40,000 ÷ ₹110) × 1,000 = 363,636
↓
Impressions × CTR = Clickse.g. 363,636 × 0.012 = 4,364 clicks
↓
Impressions × Lead Rate = Leadse.g. 363,636 × 0.009 = 3,273 leads
↓
Budget ÷ Leads = CPLe.g. ₹40,000 ÷ 3,273 = ₹12 CPL
Additional Meta formulas
Reach = Impressions ÷ Frequency
CPC = CPM ÷ (CTR × 10)
Lead Rate = Form Open Rate × Form Submit Rate → typically 0.5–2%
🟡 Universal Formulas
Works on all platforms
CVR (%) = (Conversions ÷ Clicks) × 100
CPA = Budget ÷ Acquisitions
ROAS = Revenue Generated ÷ Ad Spend
ROI (%) = ((Revenue − Total Cost) ÷ Total Cost) × 100
Frequency = Impressions ÷ Reach
eCPM = (Total Spend ÷ Total Impressions) × 1,000
Break-even CPL = Commission × Lead→Disburse Rate
Revenue = Leads × Lead→Disburse Rate × Avg. Commission
⚡ CPL Shortcut (most useful in client conversations):
CPL = CPC ÷ CVR
e.g. ₹200 CPC ÷ 5% CVR = ₹4,000 CPL

To halve CPL → either halve CPC (hard, needs QS improvement) OR double CVR (easier, landing page optimisation)
🎯 KPIs vs. Metrics — The Difference

A metric is any data point you can measure. A KPI is the specific metric your campaign is judged against — it has a target, a timeframe, and an owner.

Common mistake: Listing 12 KPIs. A campaign should have ONE primary KPI (the north star) and 3–5 secondary KPIs. More than that and nothing gets optimized.
Primary KPI: Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL) — a lead that passes basic qualification
Secondary KPIs: CPL, CTR, CVR, Impression Share — the levers that drive the primary KPI
Business KPIs: Disbursements, Commission, ROAS — require offline conversion import

📈 What Forecasting Really Is

Forecasting is not guessing. It is applying verified benchmarks to your budget using the correct formula chain, then stress-testing the output at Conservative, Base, and Optimistic scenarios.

🔵 Google Ads Worked Example

Personal Loan campaign, Bengaluru, Month 1 · Budget = ₹30,000

Clicks = ₹30,000 ÷ ₹130 (CPC) = 230 clicks
Impr. = 230 ÷ 0.042 (CTR) = 5,476 impr.
Leads = 230 × 0.048 (CVR) = 11 leads
CPL = ₹30,000 ÷ 11 = ₹2,727
CPL check = ₹130 ÷ 0.048 = ₹2,708 ✓
🔴 Meta Ads Worked Example

Home Loan Lead Ad, Bengaluru + Mumbai, Month 1 · Budget = ₹40,000

Impr. = (₹40,000 ÷ ₹110) × 1000 = 363,636
Clicks = 363,636 × 0.012 (CTR) = 4,364
Leads = 363,636 × 0.009 (LR) = 3,273
CPL = ₹40,000 ÷ 3,273 = ₹12.22

Meta Lead Ads use a lead rate (leads per impression) because the form opens inside the platform — no landing page click required. Lead Rate = form open rate × form submit rate. Typically 0.5–1.5% for India finance.

⚠️ 3-Scenario Stress Test — The Professional Standard

Always present Conservative, Base, and Optimistic scenarios. This demonstrates competence and protects you when reality differs from plan.

🔍 Where to Source Benchmark Inputs

The foundation of any forecast is verified benchmarks. Never use made-up numbers — always cite your source.

🔵 Google Keyword Planner
Search volume, CPC range, competition level per keyword
→ Google Ads CPC benchmarks
📊 WordStream Industry Reports
CTR, CVR, CPC, CPL by industry (updated annually)
→ Google Ads sector benchmarks
🔴 Meta Ads Manager
Audience Size tool — estimated reach, CPM range for a defined audience
→ Meta CPM and reach estimates
🇮🇳 upGrowth / Think with Google
India-specific NBFC/lending benchmark data and case studies
→ India finance sector benchmarks
📁 Your Own Account Data
Historical performance from your ad accounts — the most accurate benchmark available
→ Months 2+ planning
🔎 Meta Ad Library
Competitor creative strategy, offer positioning, ad formats in market
→ Creative benchmarking
